Brendan Eich

Creator of JavaScript • Co-Founder of Mozilla • CEO of Brave

An American technologist best known for creating JavaScript and shaping the modern web ecosystem.

1961 — Early Life

Brendan Eich was born on July 4, 1961, in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, United States. He developed an early interest in mathematics, programming logic, and computer systems.

Education

He earned a Bachelor's degree in Mathematics and Computer Science from Santa Clara University. Later, he completed his Master’s degree in Computer Science at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

Early Career

Before Netscape, Eich worked at Silicon Graphics and other technology companies, focusing on systems programming and compiler technologies.

1995 — Creation of JavaScript

While working at Netscape Communications, Eich was tasked with developing a scripting language for the browser. In approximately 10 days, he created JavaScript.

Originally named Mocha, then LiveScript, it was later renamed JavaScript. The language enabled dynamic web pages and transformed the internet permanently.

1998 — Mozilla Foundation

Eich co-founded Mozilla and played a central role in the open-source movement. He contributed significantly to the development of Firefox and the standardization of ECMAScript.

2014 — Mozilla CEO

In 2014, he briefly served as CEO of Mozilla. However, due to controversy surrounding past political donations, he resigned shortly after his appointment.

2015 — Founding Brave Software

In 2015, Eich founded Brave Software. Brave introduced a privacy-focused browser that blocks trackers by default and launched Basic Attention Token (BAT) as a blockchain-based advertising model.

Present — Ongoing Influence

As of today, Brendan Eich continues to serve as CEO of Brave. JavaScript remains one of the most widely used programming languages globally, powering frontend frameworks, backend systems (Node.js), mobile apps, and Web3 technologies.
