Brendan Eich

Early Life (1961)

Brendan Eich was born on July 4, 1961, in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, United States. From an early age, he showed strong interest in mathematics and computer systems.

The Birth of JavaScript (1995)

In 1995, while working at Netscape, he was tasked with creating a scripting language for the web browser. In approximately 10 days, JavaScript was created.

Originally called Mocha, then LiveScript, it was finally renamed JavaScript.

Mozilla Foundation

Eich co-founded Mozilla and played a key role in the development of Firefox and the standardization of ECMAScript.

In 2015, he founded Brave Software, a privacy-focused browser company. Brave blocks trackers by default and introduced the Basic Attention Token (BAT).

Legacy

JavaScript has become the dominant programming language of the modern web. It powers frontend interfaces, backend systems (Node.js), mobile applications, and Web3 technologies.
